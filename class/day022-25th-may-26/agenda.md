## introduction to javascript:

- where to load the JS file in html ( head or inside body)

- var, let, const keywords:
  - `var` — function-scoped; can be redeclared and reassigned; hoisted and initialized to `undefined`.
  - `let` — block-scoped; cannot be redeclared in the same scope but can be reassigned; hoisted into a temporal dead zone until initialized.
  - `const` — block-scoped; must be initialized at declaration; cannot be redeclared or reassigned; hoisted into a temporal dead zone until initialized.

- hoisting in JavaScript:
  - Declarations are moved to the top of their scope (hoisted).
  - `var` declarations are hoisted and initialized with `undefined`.
  - `let` and `const` declarations are hoisted but not initialized; accessing them before initialization throws a `ReferenceError` (Temporal Dead Zone).
